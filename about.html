Personal

As mentioned on the home page, I am an United States Marine Corps veteran that was enlisted as an avionics technician a focus on radar technologies for F/A-18C aircraft for 5 years. I've always had a passion for troubleshooting, creating, and entertaining others. I decided to become a software developer because since I was in high school first learning HTML, I fell in love with creating something from nothing with just being able to type text into a field and watch it grow.

After leaving the USMC, I worked as a technician for AT&T installing internet in the Appalachians of North Carolina. I made a career change and became a maintenance electrician for a factory in Northern Indiana before moving to Indianapolis to once again be an avionics technician again. Through the pandemic, I found myself laid off and set forth on a new career as an In-Home Expert for AT&T, which involved a mix of customer relations as well as sales, which I excelled at with no previous sales experience.

During my role in sales, I quickly learned that even though I enjoyed communicating with customers daily and was great at it, I missed working with my hands. I missed troublehsooting, solving problems, and making something come to life. And so I found my way into devCodeCamp, where I studied full-stack development.
